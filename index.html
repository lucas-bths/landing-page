Who am I?

I'm a freshman at Yale who has a strong interest in machine learning and mathematics. I've previously worked as a data analyst intern at ConEdison, a deep learning research intern at Stevens Tech, and an ML Engineer at SpeakEZ and Mancer Robotics.

I conduct machine learnng research in my spare time, having pursued projects in socioeconomic well-being, skin cancer detection, and stutter classification. I have developed my own Neural Network architectures to compete against state-of-the-art learning models.

Aside from my love for math and ML, I'm an avid tennis player, I'm active in my Boy Scout troop as an Eagle Scout, and I love to lead neighborhood rennovation projects at my local park.

What are my skills?

My passion for Machine Learning grew after I read the book "Genius Makers" by Cade Metz and learned I can use my love for mathematics to make positive changes in the world through developing ML algorithms. After conducting multiple ML research projects, I’ve honed proficiency in Python, along with ML-related libraries such as NumPy, Scikit Learn, TensorFlow, and PyTorch. I’ve also fostered invaluable data analytical and problem-solving skills through these projects, whether choosing the proper ML architecture to complete the data or deciding what modifications to make to the given dataset to maximize model performance.

Additionally, my work in the scientific and corporate world has allowed me to grow my attitude of gracious professionalism, eagerly collaborating with my peers on projects from detecting coronary artery calcification to analyzing electrical structures in need of repair in NYC via geolocation tools. My experience with balancing my work and personal life has enabled me to better appreciate the efforts of my colleagues and become more attentive, considerate, and flexible to their requests for assistance in the workplace.

What is my experience?

A brief sample of my research projects.

A Quantitative Analysis of Global Well-being

I analyze and predict the impact that socioeconomic indicators have on populational well-being by training ML models, from Multivariate Regression to Neural Networks, on quantitative data from the World Bank and United Nations. I presented my research at the Terra NYC STEM Fair and the Junior Science and Humanities Symposium, earning Second and First Place, respectively.
